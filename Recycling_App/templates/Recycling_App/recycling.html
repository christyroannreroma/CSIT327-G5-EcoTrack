üìú Republic Act No. 9003 - Ecological Solid Waste Management Act

The Philippines' national framework for solid waste management, enforced in Cebu City

üèõÔ∏è Key Provisions

  • Segregation at Source: All residents must segregate waste into biodegradable, non-biodegradable, recyclable, and special waste
  • Mandatory Composting: Households and establishments must compost biodegradable waste
  • No Burning of Waste: Open burning of solid waste is strictly prohibited
  • Extended Producer Responsibility: Manufacturers must manage post-consumer waste of their products
  • Ban on Non-Environmentally Acceptable Products: Certain plastic products and packaging are prohibited

üÜï Republic Act No. 11898 - Extended Producer Responsibility Act (2022)

Manufacturers now responsible for plastic waste recovery

  • Large Enterprises: Companies must recover plastic packaging waste equivalent to 20% (2023) increasing to 80% by 2028
  • Producer Responsibility: Manufacturers, importers, and brands must establish collection and recycling systems
  • Single-Use Plastics: Gradual phase-out of non-essential single-use plastics
  • Local Implementation: Cebu City coordinates with EPR-compliant companies for waste collection
  • Consumer Role: Return packaging to designated collection points or retail stores

For Households

üìÑ Paper & Cardboard

Paper products are highly recyclable and help save trees.

  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Office paper and documents
  • Cardboard boxes (flattened)
  • Paper bags
  • Junk mail and envelopes

ü•§ Plastics

Check the recycling number on plastic items (1, 2, and 5 are most recyclable).

  • Plastic bottles (PET #1)
  • Milk jugs (HDPE #2)
  • Yogurt containers (PP #5)
  • Detergent bottles
  • Plastic bags (at designated drop-offs)

üçæ Glass

Glass can be recycled endlessly without losing quality.

  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Food containers
  • Beverage bottles
  • Must be clean and label-free
  • No window glass or mirrors

ü•´ Metals

Aluminum and steel are valuable recyclable materials.

  • Aluminum cans
  • Steel food cans
  • Tin foil (clean)
  • Metal bottle caps
  • Empty aerosol cans

üíª Electronics

E-waste requires special recycling to recover valuable materials.

  • Computers and laptops
  • Mobile phones
  • Tablets and e-readers
  • Cables and chargers
  • Take to e-waste collection centers

üîã Batteries

Batteries contain hazardous materials and must be recycled properly.

  • Rechargeable batteries
  • Single-use alkaline batteries
  • Button cell batteries
  • Car batteries
  • Never throw in regular trash

üå± Recycling Tips

Clean Before Recycling

Rinse containers to remove food residue. Contaminated items can ruin entire recycling batches.

Remove Labels & Caps

Most labels can stay on, but remove caps and lids as they're often made from different materials.

Flatten Cardboard

Break down boxes to save space in recycling bins and make transportation more efficient.

Know Your Numbers

Learn the plastic recycling symbols (1-7) and check what your local facility accepts.

Don't Bag Recyclables

Place items loose in the bin. Plastic bags can jam sorting machinery.

When in Doubt, Throw it Out

If you're unsure if something is recyclable, it's better to trash it than contaminate recycling.
